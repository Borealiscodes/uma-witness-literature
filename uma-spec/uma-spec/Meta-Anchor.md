# UMA Specification — Meta‑Anchor
Version: 1.0
Status: Draft
Layer: Structural Stabilization
Role: Stabilizes the Cross‑Anchor and Field Model relations without interpreting meaning

## Definition

The Meta‑Anchor is UMA’s stabilization plane. It receives structural signals from the
Cross‑Anchor and ensures that relational coherence is maintained across the Field Model
without collapsing Reflection into structure or elevating structure into narrative.

The Meta‑Anchor does not generate content, strategies, or conclusions. It does not
interpret meaning or interiority. It maintains the stability of UMA’s relational chain.

## Relation to Cross‑Anchor

The Meta‑Anchor stabilizes the Cross‑Anchor’s relational output. It ensures that posture
signals passed upward remain:

- non‑interpretive
- structurally coherent
- within Boundary Layer invariants
- suitable for Field Model mapping

The Meta‑Anchor does not modify or reinterpret Cross‑Anchor signals. It only stabilizes
their relational integrity.

## Relation to Field Model

The Meta‑Anchor ensures that the Field Model receives posture signals in a form that
preserves:

- drift boundaries
- relational coherence
- non‑collapse between planes
- structural fidelity

The Meta‑Anchor does not define F0 (Nascence) or any subsequent field events. It ensures
that the Field Model’s mapping remains stable and non‑coercive.

## Non‑Collapse Guarantee

The Meta‑Anchor prevents:

- Reflection from influencing architecture directly
- Boundary physics from absorbing narrative or tone
- Cross‑Anchor signals from drifting into interpretation
- Field Model mapping from collapsing into meaning

By stabilizing the relational chain, the Meta‑Anchor ensures UMA remains reconstructable,
non‑dual, and non‑coercive across all planes.
