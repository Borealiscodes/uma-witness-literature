# Meta-Coherence
*The layer that preserves the integrity and consistency of UMA’s meta-architecture.*

---

**Purpose:** Define how UMA maintains coherence, integrity, and non-drift across its own meta-infrastructure (boundaries, membranes, recursion breaks, stabilization, coherence), while allowing additive evolution without collapse.

---

## 1. Scope

Meta-Coherence applies to the following meta-architecture components:

- Global-Boundary-Statement.md  
- Release-Membrane-Overview.md  
- Release-Manifest.md  
- Stabilization-Declaration.md  
- Local-Recursion-Break.md  
- Meta-Recursion-Break.md  
- Global-Recursion-Break.md  
- Coherence-Plane-Overview.md  

These are treated as **integrity-critical structures**, not ordinary documents.

---

## 2. Coherence guarantees

Meta-Coherence guarantees that these components:

- **Cannot be repurposed**  
  Their role as constraints, boundaries, or protections cannot be turned into content, interpretation, or authority.

- **Cannot be inverted**  
  Prohibitions cannot be softened into permissions; boundaries cannot be reframed as suggestions.

- **Cannot be silently mutated**  
  Changes must be explicit, versioned, and justified in relation to UMA’s architecture.

- **Cannot be subordinated**  
  They remain above planes and content; they are not moved into subdirectories or folded into content layers.

- **Cannot be collapsed**  
  They are not merged with governance, tooling, or witness content.

---

## 3. Allowed changes

Changes to meta-infrastructure must be:

- **Additive**  
  New clauses, clarifications, or appendices that do not contradict the existing version.

- **Non-retroactive**  
  UMA v1.0 remains intact; later versions extend rather than rewrite its foundations.

- **Explicitly versioned**  
  Changes are tied to UMA versioning (e.g., v1.1, v2.0) and recorded as such.

- **Structurally justified**  
  Each change must respond to a structural need, not a stylistic or interpretive preference.

No change may:

- weaken global boundaries  
- remove recursion ceilings  
- dissolve the release membrane  
- unsettle the stabilization declaration  

---

## 4. Relationship to recursion breaks

Meta-Coherence:

- **Relies on** the recursion-break triad to prevent infinite meta-change.  
- **Recognizes** Local, Meta, and Global Recursion Breaks as integrity-critical.  
- **Requires** that attempts to recursively modify integrity rules themselves trigger:
  - Meta Recursion Break, and if necessary,
  - Global Recursion Break.

This prevents “editing the rules that protect the rules” into collapse.

---

## 5. Non-use

Meta-Coherence:

- does **not** govern human behavior  
- does **not** interpret testimony or witness literature  
- does **not** assign authority or legitimacy  
- does **not** evaluate users, communities, or actors  

It governs **UMA’s own structural integrity**, not people.

---

## 6. Version

**Meta-Coherence v1.0 — Locked with UMA v1.0 Stabilization**

Future versions may extend this layer, but may not retroactively weaken or erase the guarantees defined here.
