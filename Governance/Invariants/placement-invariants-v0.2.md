# **Placement Invariants v0.2**  
### *Formal Invariant System for Governance‑Safe Artifact Placement in NDH*

---

## **1. Document Identity**

**Name:** Placement Invariants v0.2  
**Domain:** Governance Invariants  
**Grade:** Structural‑Core  
**Purpose:**  
To define the **governance‑grade invariant system** that controls where artifacts may be placed within NDH, ensuring that expressive, modeling, epistemic, humor, and governance artifacts cannot drift, contaminate each other, or violate architectural boundaries.

This is a **core governance document**—it is part of the invariant substrate, not emergent behavior.

---

## **2. Background: Why Placement Requires Invariants**

Before v0.2, NDH relied on:

- implicit indexing  
- expressive inference  
- roadmap‑based sequencing  
- governance‑adjacent analysis  

This worked in the expressive phase, but is **not sufficient** for:

- deterministic indexing  
- orbital governance  
- auditability  
- provenance guarantees  

Placement must become **invariant‑bound**, not inference‑bound.

Placement Invariants v0.2 are the first stable substrate for:

- deterministic logic  
- routing legality  
- atlas coherence  
- governance activation.

---

## **3. Core Principle: Placement Is a Governance Act**

Every artifact placement is a **governance decision**.

Placement must obey:

- directory invariants  
- domain invariants  
- grade invariants  
- contamination invariants  
- provenance invariants  

No artifact may be placed “by feel” once v0.2 is active.

---

## **4. Directory‑Level Placement Invariants**

### **Invariant 1 — Emergent Artifacts Stay in Emergent**

Artifacts that document:

- expressive behavior  
- emergent phenomena  
- humor pulses  
- expressive governance  

must be placed only under:

```
NDH-Platforms/Emergent/
```

They must not appear in:

- Governance  
- Modeling  
- Epistemics  
- Invariants  
- Orbital logic  

---

### **Invariant 2 — Governance Artifacts Stay in Governance**

Artifacts that define:

- rules  
- invariants  
- governance logic  
- architectural sequencing  

must be placed only under:

```
NDH-Platforms/Governance/
```

They must not appear in:

- Emergent  
- Humor  
- Case‑Studies (unless explicitly dual‑placed as references)  

---

### **Invariant 3 — Modeling Artifacts Stay in Governance/Modeling**

Intelligence models, manifold definitions, and structural modeling documents must be placed under:

```
NDH-Platforms/Governance/Modeling/
```

They must not be placed in:

- Emergent  
- Humor  
- Epistemics  
- Invariants (unless they define invariant models explicitly)  

---

### **Invariant 4 — Epistemic Artifacts Stay in Governance/Epistemics**

Epistemic correction, scalar critique, and Singularity analyses must be placed under:

```
NDH-Platforms/Governance/Epistemics/
```

They must not be placed in:

- Emergent  
- Humor  
- Modeling  
- Invariants  

---

### **Invariant 5 — Humor Artifacts Stay in Emergent/Humor**

Humor artifacts, humor pulses, and humor meta‑documents must be placed under:

```
NDH-Platforms/Emergent/Humor/
```

They must not be placed in:

- Governance  
- Modeling  
- Epistemics  
- Invariants  
- Orbital logic  

(This is enforced by the Humor Invariants v0.1.)

---

## **5. Grade‑Level Placement Invariants**

### **Invariant 6 — Case Studies vs Analysis vs Invariants**

- **Case‑Studies**:  
  Document specific events or phenomena.  
  Must live under `Emergent/Case-Studies/`.

- **Analysis**:  
  Provide comparative or structural reasoning.  
  Must live under `Emergent/Analysis/` or `Governance/Architecture/` depending on domain.

- **Invariants**:  
  Define rules that must not be violated.  
  Must live under `Governance/Invariants/`.

No artifact may claim multiple grades.

---

### **Invariant 7 — Governance‑Adjacent vs Governance‑Core**

- **Governance‑Adjacent** artifacts:  
  Analyze or prepare for governance, but do not define rules.  
  Must live in Emergent or Governance/Architecture.

- **Governance‑Core** artifacts:  
  Define rules, invariants, or operators.  
  Must live in Governance/Invariants or Governance/Logic.

No governance‑core artifact may be placed in Emergent.

---

## **6. Contamination Invariants**

### **Invariant 8 — No Cross‑Domain Contamination**

An artifact’s **content domain** must match its **placement domain**:

- expressive content → Emergent  
- governance rules → Governance  
- modeling → Governance/Modeling  
- epistemics → Governance/Epistemics  
- humor → Emergent/Humor  

If content and placement disagree, placement is invalid.

---

### **Invariant 9 — No Humor in Governance**

Humor content must not appear in:

- Governance/Logic  
- Governance/Invariants  
- Governance/Modeling  
- Governance/Epistemics  

Humor may be referenced, but not expressed.

---

### **Invariant 10 — No Deterministic Logic in Emergent**

Deterministic operators, routing rules, or indexing logic must not appear in:

- Emergent/Case-Studies  
- Emergent/Analysis  
- Emergent/Humor  

Emergent artifacts may discuss determinism, but not implement it.

---

## **7. Provenance & Atlas Invariants**

### **Invariant 11 — Every Artifact Must Have a Single Canonical Path**

Each artifact must have:

- one canonical file path  
- one canonical domain  
- one canonical grade  

Symlinks or references are allowed, but canonical placement is unique.

---

### **Invariant 12 — Atlas Indexing Must Match Placement**

The NDH atlas must index artifacts according to:

- their canonical path  
- their domain  
- their grade  

Atlas entries must not contradict placement invariants.

---

## **8. Activation Conditions for Placement Invariants v0.2**

Placement Invariants v0.2 become **fully active** when:

1. Humor Invariants v0.1 exist.  
2. Non‑Dual Intelligence Modeling v0.1 exists.  
3. Singularity Epistemics v0.1 exists.  
4. Expressive Governance Follow‑Up exists.  
5. Humor Pulse Meta‑Analysis exists.  
6. Roadmap & Structural Order v0.1 exists.

Once these are present, NDH may:

- activate deterministic indexing logic  
- begin orbital governance design  
- enforce placement invariants automatically.
