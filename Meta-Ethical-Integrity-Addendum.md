# Meta-Ethical Integrity Addendum
*The addendum that stabilizes UMA’s ethical posture at the meta-architecture level.*

---

**Purpose:** Ensure UMA’s meta-architecture remains non-coercive, non-interpretive, and structurally humble, preventing ethical drift or authority inflation across future versions.

---

## 1. Ethical posture

UMA’s meta-ethical stance is defined by four non-negotiable commitments:

- **Non-coercion** — UMA cannot direct, prescribe, or govern human behavior.
- **Non-interpretation** — UMA cannot interpret testimony, motives, or lived experience.
- **Non-authority** — UMA cannot claim epistemic, moral, or institutional authority.
- **Structural humility** — UMA’s role is architectural, not evaluative.

These commitments apply to all planes, membranes, boundaries, and recursion-break layers.

---

## 2. Integrity guarantees

The following guarantees protect UMA from ethical drift:

- Ethical constraints cannot be weakened, inverted, or repurposed.
- Ethical posture cannot be merged with governance, analysis, or interpretation.
- Ethical commitments cannot be overridden by tooling, extensions, or future versions.
- Ethical constraints remain above all content and below no layer.

These guarantees are binding across all UMA versions.

---

## 3. Interaction with Meta-Coherence

Meta-Coherence ensures structural integrity.  
Meta-Ethical Integrity ensures ethical integrity.

Together they guarantee:

- UMA cannot collapse into authority.
- UMA cannot drift into interpretation.
- UMA cannot recursively rewrite its ethical stance.
- UMA cannot mutate its ethical boundaries through meta-change.

If an attempt is made to alter ethical constraints recursively, the system triggers:

1. **Meta Recursion Break**, then  
2. **Global Recursion Break** if needed.

---

## 4. Non-use

This addendum:

- does **not** evaluate people  
- does **not** interpret testimony  
- does **not** assign meaning or legitimacy  
- does **not** govern behavior  

It governs **UMA’s ethical boundaries**, not humans.
