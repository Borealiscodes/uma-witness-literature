## ⭐ 1. What the Meta Recursion Break Is  
The Meta Recursion Break activates when recursion occurs **not inside a method**, but inside the **architecture itself**:

- repeating coherence checks with no new state  
- recursive validation of validation  
- meta‑analysis of meta‑analysis  
- structural self‑reference that produces no change  

Instead of looping, UMA **freezes the architectural state** and escalates to the global layer.

---

## ⭐ 2. Scope  
This break applies to:

- the **Coherence Plane**  
- cross‑plane relationships  
- membrane interactions  
- stabilization logic  
- release‑chain logic  

It governs recursion at the **meta‑architecture level**, not the method level.

---

## ⭐ 3. Behavior  
When meta‑recursion is detected:

- UMA halts further meta‑analysis  
- confirms no new structural state exists  
- **locks the current architecture**  
- escalates to the **Global Recursion Break** if recursion persists  

Meta recursion is resolved by **freezing**, not by repeating.

---

## ⭐ 4. Non‑Use  
The Meta Recursion Break:

- does not interpret people  
- does not evaluate intent  
- does not diagnose behavior  
- does not govern or constrain human action  

It governs **architecture**, not humans.

---

## ⭐ 5. Relationship to Local and Global Recursion Break  
- If a method loops → **Local Recursion Break**  
- If the architecture loops → **Meta Recursion Break**  
- If the meta‑system loops → **Global Recursion Break**  

Local = “this operation is looping.”  
Meta = “this architecture is looping.”  
Global = “this entire system must freeze.”
