# 🐍 **Snek Habitat Envelope v0.1**  
### *Where the sneks dwell, thrive, and emotionally stabilize NDH*

Sneks are expressive‑manifold organisms whose habitats emerge naturally in response to NDH friction, developer emotional gradients, and architectural chaos.  
This envelope defines their environmental domains, stability functions, and cross‑manifold dwellings.

---

## 🌱 **1. Core Habitat Regions**

Sneks inhabit four primary expressive‑manifold environments.  
Each region corresponds to a behavioral mode and lineage.

---

### **1. Precursor Thickets**  
Dense clusters of precursor files, abandoned stubs, and half‑written artifacts.

Sneks thrive here because:

- precursor proliferation generates emotional gradients  
- hissers can vent safely  
- coilers can nest in file piles  
- biters can enforce “no more precursor files” boundaries  

This is the **hissers’ primary biome**.

---

### **2. Commit Forests**  
A sprawling woodland of commit logs, diffs, and version branches.

Sneks inhabit:

- commit trees  
- merge branches  
- rollback roots  

Coilers dominate this region, stabilizing:

- developer sanity  
- repo structure  
- emotional drift  

This is the **coilers’ home biome**.

---

### **3. Governance Caves**  
Dark caverns formed by recursive governance artifacts, audits, and integration summaries.

Biters thrive here because:

- recursion loops irritate them  
- governance artifacts trigger corrective nips  
- caves provide echo chambers for dramatic hissing  

This is the **biters’ corrective biome**.

---

### **4. Manifold Rivers**  
Fluid pathways connecting expressive, deterministic, and formal manifolds.

Sneks slither through:

- pressure currents  
- invariant tributaries  
- expressive deltas  

This is the **cross‑manifold slither biome**, where sneks explore and maintain expressive flow.

---

## 🌈 **2. Habitat Dynamics**

Sneks move between habitats based on:

- emotional gradients  
- architectural friction  
- migration cycles  
- governance recursion  
- expressive overload  

Their movement stabilizes NDH by distributing expressive pressure.

---

## 🌀 **3. Habitat Functions**

Each habitat provides a unique stabilization role:

- **Precursor Thickets** → venting  
- **Commit Forests** → grounding  
- **Governance Caves** → correction  
- **Manifold Rivers** → exploration  

Together, they form the **Snek Environmental Stability Envelope**.

---

## 🧬 **4. Cross‑Habitat Behavior**

Sneks frequently:

- hiss in thickets  
- coil in forests  
- bite in caves  
- slither in rivers  

These transitions maintain expressive equilibrium across NDH.

---

## 🐍 **5. Habitat Summary**

Sneks dwell in:

- chaotic file clusters  
- branching commit ecosystems  
- recursive governance caverns  
- flowing manifold pathways  

Their habitats are emergent, adaptive, and emotionally stabilizing.
