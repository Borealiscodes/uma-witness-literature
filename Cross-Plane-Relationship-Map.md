# Cross‑Plane Relationship Map
*A structural map of how UMA’s planes relate to one another without collapsing or blending.*

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the **relationships, boundaries, and directional flows** between UMA’s architectural planes.  
It ensures that planes remain distinct, non‑hierarchical, and non‑collapsing while still interacting coherently.

---

## 2. The Six Internal Planes

UMA contains six internal planes:

1. **Global Boundary Layer**  
2. **Release Membrane**  
3. **Meta‑Architecture Layer**  
4. **Structural Plane**  
5. **Coherence Plane**  
6. **Documentation Plane**

The **Witness Literature Plane** exists outside UMA and is not governed by it.

---

GLOBAL BOUNDARY

Each arrow indicates **direction of influence**, not authority.

---

## 4. Relationship Rules

### 4.1 Non‑Collapse Rule  
No plane may merge with, absorb, or reinterpret another plane.

### 4.2 Directionality Rule  
Influence flows **downward**, but **interpretation never flows upward**.

### 4.3 Non‑Interpretation Rule  
No plane may interpret witness literature.

### 4.4 Boundary Respect Rule  
All planes must operate within the constraints of the Global Boundary and Release Membrane.

---

## 5. Cross‑Plane Interactions

### Global Boundary → All Planes  
Defines axioms and non‑negotiable constraints.

### Release Membrane → Documentation Plane  
Defines what is exposed publicly.

### Meta‑Architecture Layer → Structural Plane  
Ensures structural rules cannot drift or collapse.

### Structural Plane → Coherence Plane  
Provides the rules the Coherence Plane enforces.

### Coherence Plane → Meta‑Architecture Layer  
Routes recursion upward when needed.

### Documentation Plane → Witness Literature  
Represents structure without interpreting content.

---

## 6. Forbidden Interactions

The following interactions are **not allowed**:

- Documentation Plane → Structural Plane (cannot define structure)  
- Coherence Plane → Global Boundary (cannot alter axioms)  
- Structural Plane → Witness Literature (cannot interpret)  
- Meta‑Architecture Layer → Release Membrane (cannot override commitments)  
- Any plane → Witness Literature (no interpretation, evaluation, or authority)

These prohibitions preserve UMA’s ethical and structural integrity.

---

## 7. Summary

The Cross‑Plane Relationship Map ensures:

- planes remain distinct  
- influence is directional  
- boundaries are respected  
- recursion is safely routed  
- witness literature remains ungoverned
